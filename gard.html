Gard










I designed Gard as a final project for my COGS 122 Interaction Design Startup class. In teams of 5, we came up with a business startup idea. We designed a peer-to-peer drop storage idea that would let users conveniently store and pick up their belongings where they want by utilizing other users' existing storage space.
My role:

- research (observations, interviews)
- storyboarding
- prototype testing (service reenactments)
- value flow model







Phase 1: Ideation








Problem



Through user interviews and observations, we focused on these problems:


- Inconveniently carry belongings. Many of our peers had to carry around burdensome items for long periods of time throughout their day.

- Lack of available public storage space. Storage space only comes as storage units.

- Available private storage space. We noticed that many people had storage space in their private property.





Storyboarding



We ideated further to flesh out potential solutions to our problem space of storage and commuting. One of the storyboards (shown above) captured the concept of "drop storage", a shared economy model that connected existing private storage spaces with people's burdensome, yet necessary belongings.



Value Flow Model

Next, I made a value flow model that illustrated how our startup idea would generate revenue. Our startup idea used the concept of a "shared economy", since users were renting out space temporarily from others. Our startup idea was a platform that provided users the ability to rent out temporary storage space for their inconvenient belongings from other users rather than going to storage compounds. With our idea coming into focus, we moved on to the next step of prototyping.







Phase 2: Prototyping

Task #2

From the first service encounter, we learned that people felt comfortable storing other people's personal items as long as the item was not too large or cumbersome. In addition, users felt more comfortable with the service when they were able to see the storage space. We considered implementing a proof of storage space concept for potential storage spaces to be verified, so users could view pictures of the space beforehand. Another thing we learned was that asking storers to take on the role of deliveryfor drop-off/pick-up of items was too much to expect. Lastly, users felt comfortable using the service for items that were not too valuable and replaceable.


From the second service encounter, we learned that people are willing to part with valuable personal items if carrying them around is not worth the effort (surfboard, camera equipment, etc.). We learned that people were willing to pay a small sum (~$6) for the service. Another thing we learned was people are patient to a certain degree in waiting for a pick-up and for a drop-off. We decided a penalty for being late would keep people accountable. In addition, a rating system for users and storers was implemented to keep people honest.


From the third service encounter, we learned multi-day storage was perceived as less secure than same-day storage. We tinkered with the concept of item insurance. Also, adding personal expertise and experience storers have handling certain types of items was noted. An option to update pick-up time by checking with the storer was added due to changes in plans.

We had to test how we wanted to present our idea. A teammate and I created step-by-step visuals that explained how to use Gard and ran A/B tests on it, while other teammates worked on logos and Invision prototypes. The A Version was much more successful due to its simple "problem -> solution" timeline.






Project Submission







The final project was a presentation where we would make a pitch to investors that had come. We made a short film that illustrated our problem space and solution. Hypothetically, the next steps would have been to start prototyping a high-fidelity mobile app for users to find Gards while also refining the drop storage idea. Specifically, safeguards for user's possessions along with other's privacy and safety concerns would need to be addressed clearly first, before being able to proceed with the idea further. This project really pushed me to explore problem spaces from a different perspective in order to come up with unique solutions that were somewhat feasible.
